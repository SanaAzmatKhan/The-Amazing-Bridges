BRIDGE

A bridge is a structure built to span physical obstacles without closing the way underneath such as a body of water, valley, or road, for the purpose of providing passage over the obstacle. Bridges can be categorized in several different ways.
1: Fixed or movable bridges.
2: Double-decked bridges.
3: Three-way bridges.
4: Structure type.
5: Viaducts.

Did You Know !

GOLDEN GATE BRIDGE

Eleven men died during the construction of the Golden Gate Bridge.

TOWER BRIDGE

Tower Bridge was originally chocolate brown but was painted red, white and blue in 1977 to celebrate The Queen's Silver Jubilee.

TSING MA BRIDGE

It is the world's 11th-longest span suspension bridge. The bridge was named after two of the islands at its ends, namely Tsing Yi and Ma Wan.

CONFEDERATION BRIDGE

It is the longest bridge over ice-covered waters in the world. Bridge type is Box girder bridge.

AKASHI KAIKO BRIDGE

Akashi Kaikyō Bridge is the seventh-tallest bridge by structural height at 282.8 metres (928 ft).

Széchenyi Chain Bridge

At the time of its construction, Chain Bridge was considered to be one of the wonders of the world.

DANYANG–KUNSHAN BRIDGE

The Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge is a 164.8-kilometre long viaduct on the Beijing–Shanghai High-Speed Railway. It is the world's longest bridge.

PALACE BRIDGE

Palace Bridge, a road- and foot-traffic bascule bridge, spans the Neva River in Saint Petersburg between Palace Square and Vasilievsky Island.

THE NANPU BRIDGE

The Nanpu Bridge, in Shanghai, China, sister bridge to the Yangpu Bridge, is one of the main bridges in Shanghai.
